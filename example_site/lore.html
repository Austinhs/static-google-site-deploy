THE GREAT FLOOD

THE GREAT FLOOD

In the years before the world’s waters washed through all of civilization, conflict was common among three of the original Bloodlines. The family bond between Songhai and Agarthian societies had gone sour. Meanwhile, Atlantean society had been captivated with a lust for rare resources, causing them to become contentious toward seafaring Songhai clans.

A rift had severed the fruitful relationship of the Songhai and Agarthians. And, it all began as a result of a single trading disagreement. Certain Songhai mercantilists, with the full support of their superiors, had begun price gouging in their trade of metals, hides, and medicines to the Agarthians. Some of these wares, unable to be found underground, had become essential for Agarthian society. While only a dispute between two trading networks, leadership quickly caught wind of the events. Representatives from both Bloodlines soon met, only for their negotiations to become increasingly hostile.

The rest was history. Songhai troops marched upon the underworld, where they were met by vast Agarthian armies. A brutal battlefield had formed at the precipice of the underground. This was where Songhai and Agarthian troops clashed, causing great bloodshed for years on end. Below the ocean’s expanse, Atlantean society had progressed further into its clandestine brilliance. With their basic needs met, the Atlanteans placed a new priority on the acquisition of land resources. As controllers of the seas, they began to prey on Songhai trading vessels as their primary source, ambushing them at will. Some ships were merely looted, while others were ravaged and sunk.

News of this reached Songhai leaders, who demanded all future fleets be equipped with advanced weaponry to deter the increased Atlantean threat. Cannons, complex armaments, vats of incendiary oil, and more would be used to defend the Songhai armada. This intensified the strife by tenfold. The oceans were soon polluted by these frequent skirmishes, their waters set ablaze. Combined with the ongoing Agarthian and Songhai conflict, the entire world had fallen into turmoil.

He watched as his creation spiraled into hatred, its once peaceful existence maimed with violence.

A SACRED FRUIT

The Great Flood caused legendary seeds in Eva’s garden to germinate into Golden Apples…

Deep within the vegetation of the Gardens of Eden, a terrace sat atop a lofty ridge. The sanctuary looked over the entire landmass, its entrance barred by two glorious gates. This was where Eden’s most sacred garden lay. It was also where Eva had sealed herself away in solitude, tending to what grew within.

As the Great Flood uprooted civilization, its waters unveiled Eden’s enchantment. The seawater seeped into the soil, germinating seeds that had been submerged for thousands of years. In Eva’s Garden, these seeds sprouted into something spoken of only as legend: apple trees.

The Apple Trees of Eden were a sacred spectacle. They towered in height, their sprawling branches a symbol of nature’s divinity. Their bark radiated with heat as golden grooves collided with onyx lacerations. Two distinct types of apples would share each tree’s branches.

First grew the Golden Apples, which reflected beams of light as they pulsated with celestial energy. Then grew the Bad Apples, infecting the trees with their garish and obsidian hues. These two fruits would serve as vectors for the future of civilization itself.

The Creator visited Eva, his first divine creation, and informed her of the trees’ sanctity. She was then tasked with looking after the garden in further solitude. The sanctum was to remain shielded from the outside world until further notice.

He also warned her not to eat the apples, as consuming them carried consequences. Citizens who eat Golden Apples will be gifted with the ability of ascension, ensuring their survival at the end of the Divine Tribunal. Ascended citizens will become “chosen,” and continue forward on a path toward the future.

Citizens who eat Bad Apples will sacrifice themselves to The Creator. By doing so, one of their fellow citizens will receive a Golden Apple, gaining the opportunity to be spared. At the end of each Verdict in the Divine Tribunal, citizens on the winning side are to be delivered Golden Apples. Citizens on the losing side will receive Bad Apples. The consumption of the Apples of Eden will mark the beginning of a new era.
